Resources

All resources referenced throughout the site can be found here along with further readings and websites. Click on any of these resources to be taken directly to them.

Practitioner websites

  • Portland State currently run both river restoration and advanced river restoration certificate programs with direct access to the academics and practitioners at the forefront of Stage Zero
  • A Video of the Geomorphic Grade Line explained by it's developer Paul Powers
  • The Low-Tech Process-Based Restoration (LTPBR) of Riverscapes website provides a multitude of information on one of the river restoration processes that can be used to achieve Stage Zero and/or Stage Eight
  • The UK Beavers trust has lots of resources for those interested in that aspect of Stage Zero restoration
  • Slow the Flow UK, sharing many of the same ideals and processes that make up Stage Zero
  • The Farming and Wildlife advisory group in the UK set up to understand the value of environmental land in successful farms, they have also produced a Stage Zero leaflet found here under documents
  • Mendip Flood action page contains interesting story maps on works that have been carried out with some of the goals and ideals discussed in Stage Zero
